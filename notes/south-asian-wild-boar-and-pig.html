South Asian wild boar and pig genomics

Ten years ago, in 2014, we started a project on the genomic analysis of wild boars and domestic pigs from South Asia and Southeast Asia.

The project began while I was affiliated with the College of Animal Science and Technology at Huazhong Agricultural University, PR China, and continued through my later work with the Department of Ecology and Evolution at the University of Chicago. The original question was simple, but large: what can genome-wide data tell us about wild boar origin, domestic pig history, and population structure across South Asia and Southeast Asia?

This work was fortunate to receive strong support from my mentor Dr. Shuhong Zhao, along with important academic insights from Dr. Jianlin Han and many coauthors, including Dr. Xiaoyong Du, Dr. Zhuqing Zheng, and others. Over the years, the project grew from a regional population genomics effort into a broader attempt to revisit a long-standing narrative in wild boar and pig evolutionary history.

Now, as we near completion of the manuscript, our findings suggest an alternative possibility to the traditionally accepted origins of wild boars. We present evidence supporting an Out of South Asia origin for global wild boar populations, a view that would mark a departure from the long-established historical interpretation.

The current manuscript title is: Out of South Asia migration of global wild boar: distinctive genomic profile of wild boar and domestic pig populations in South Asia.

Welcome to the South-Asian-Wild-boar-and-pig research project. The primary dataset is available on Zenodo, and the project documentation and population genomics pipeline are collected in the GitHub Wiki.
